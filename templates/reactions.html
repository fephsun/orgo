    Reactions

    Hydrogenation

    H2 cat. Pd|C in ethanol

    Regiochemistry: None

    Stereochemistry: Syn addition

    Alkenes and alkynes are candidates for this reaction. Pd|C refers to palladium on carbon, a catalyst used in hydrogenation reactions. This reaction adds two or four hydrogens to the alkene or alkyne, converting it all the way to a single bond.

    Hydrohalogenation

    HX in CH2Cl2 (either 1 equiv. or excess)

    Regiochemistry: X added to Markovnikov carbon

    Stereochemistry: None (neither syn nor anti)

    NOTE: It is possible to specify that you want to add only one equivalent of HX to the reactant. For alkynes, this results in addition only once.

    Not so much a reaction, as more of an automatic process. Hydroxyl groups attached to double bonds (enols) will tautomerize, resulting in a ketone or an aldehyde.
